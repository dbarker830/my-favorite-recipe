Stir Fry: A Land of Cooking Wonders

I love stir fry because it is such a diverse style of cooking food.
Most people think of stir fry being used in asian cuisines like in a shrimp fried rice.
A pretty generic recipe for stir fry would be white rice, a protein (such as chicken, steak, or shrimp), soy sauce, sesame oil, mirin, white/black sesame seeds, and an assortment of including broccoli, yellow squash, zucchini, and yellow onion to name a few.

Although very different in ingredients, last week I found a stir fry recipe that used prosciutto, capicola, white/jasmine rice, and a homemade basil pesto sauce, all stir fried in a wok then served. Who would've thought that some of the most common Italian ingredients on the planet would be used in stir fry?! Certainly not me, until recently.
